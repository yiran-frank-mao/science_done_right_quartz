> [!definition] Dimension
> The dimension of the configuration space is called the number of degrees of freedom.
> 

> [!theorem] Buckingham $\pi$ Theorem
> If the behaviour of a system is governed by $n$ physical variables, which depend upon $m$ fundamental independent quantities, the system evolution may be described by $n- m$ dimensionless parameter groups.

 <b><u>e.g.</u></b>  Suppose we have the following equation of motion of dimensional coordinates $\theta$ and $x$: $$ l\ddot{\theta}-\ddot{x}\cos \theta +g\sin\theta =0 $$Now define $r=\frac{x}{x_0}$ and $\tau = \frac{t}{t_0}$, then we can convert the above to the following dimensionless equation:$$l \frac{1}{t_{0}^{2}} \frac{\mathrm{d}^2\theta}{\mathrm{d}\tau^{2} }-\frac{x_0}{t_0^2} \frac{\mathrm{d}^{2}r}{\mathrm{d}\tau^{2}}\cos \theta +g\sin\theta=0$$In the above dimensionless equation we can freely set one of the coefficients.
