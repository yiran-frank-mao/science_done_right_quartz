## Analogue Fourier Computer

**Fact** The lens maps all plane waves at angle $\theta$ onto a point distance $h^\prime$ from the optical axis.

**Def**  <i><u>Fourier Plane</u></i>
At the focal plane of the lens with focal length $L_f$ we have the Fourier plane $z_F$.

**Prop** $E(x^{\prime},y^{\prime},z_{F}) \propto {\mathcal{F}}[E(x,y,0)]$
**Proof**  At Fourier plane, the amplitude of the field at some point $(x', y', z_F)$ depends on the amplitude of the plane wave components that are focused to that point. And the amplitude of the plane wave components at any given angle are found via the Fourier spectrum of the input wave. Thus
$$ E(x^{\prime},y^{\prime},z_{F}) \propto {\mathcal{F}}[E(x,y,0)]. $$
