## Introduction

Newtonian mechanics is a classical framework that describes the motion of objects based on the principles formulated by Sir Isaac Newton. Central to this theory are Newton's three laws of motion, which provide the foundation for understanding how forces affect the movement of particles and rigid bodies. The first law, the law of inertia, states that an object will remain at rest or in uniform motion unless acted upon by an external force. The second law quantitatively relates the net force acting on an object to its acceleration ($F=ma$), while the third law asserts that for every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction. This framework has been instrumental in explaining a wide range of phenomena—from the trajectories of projectiles to the motions of celestial bodies—and continues to serve as a cornerstone in both physics education and engineering applications.
