> [!definition] Einstein Tensor
> The Einstein tensor $G$ on a [[Riemannian Metrics#^c3ac13|semi-Riemannian manifold]] $(M,g)$ is defined as $$G_{ij}=R_{ij}-\frac{1}{2}g_{ij}R,$$where $R_{ij}$ is the [[Torsion and Curvature#^deb9d2|Ricci curvature tensor]] and $R$ is the Ricci scalar. 
> 

> [!proposition]
> The Einstein tensor satisfies the equation $$\nabla^i G_{ij}=0,$$where $\nabla$ is the [[The Levi-Civita Connection#^c56d8f|Levi-Civita connection]] associated with the metric $g$.
> 

## The Energy Momentum Tensor

In general relativity, continuous matter distributions and fields are described by a tensor of type $(0,2)$ called the energy-momentum tensor:

> [!definition] Energy-Momentum Tensor
> The energy-momentum tensor is a $(0,2)$-tensor $T$ such that
> - It is symmetric: $T_{ij}=T_{ji}$;
> - It vanishes on an open set $U\subseteq M$ if and only if all the matter fields vanish on $U$;
> - Obeys the equation $\nabla_{a}T^{ij}=0$.
> $\quad$ ^57fd07

> [!definition] Einstein Field Equation
> The Einstein field equation is $$G = 8\pi T,$$where $G$ is the Einstein tensor, and $T$ is the energy-momentum tensor. ^915413

> [!remark]
> The Einstein field equation gives a relation between the geometry of spacetime and the matter (energy) content of the universe.

## Vacuum Condition

> [!definition] Vacuum Space
> A vacuum space is a spacetime with no matter or energy present, i.e. $T=0$.
