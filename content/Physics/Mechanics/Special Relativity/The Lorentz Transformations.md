Based on the two postulates of special relativity, we can derive the following transformations replacing the [[The Principles of Relativity and Determinacy#^ed935d|Galilean transformations]].

> [!proposition] Lorentz Transformations
> The Lorentz transformation relates coordinates $(x, y, z, t)$ in frame $S$ to coordinates $(x', y', z', t')$ in frame $S'$ moving with velocity $v$ along the $+x$ axis. $$\begin{aligned} x' &= \gamma (x - vt) \\ y' &= y \\ z' &= z \\ t' &= \gamma \left(t - \frac{vx}{c^2}\right) \end{aligned}$$
> The following is the inverse Lorentz transformations (switch $v \to -v$ and primes):
> $$x = \gamma (x' + vt') \quad \text{and} \quad t = \gamma \left(t' + \frac{vx'}{c^2}\right)$$ ^dfb384

*Proof*
