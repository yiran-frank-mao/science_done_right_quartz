We start from the [[Universal Enveloping Algebra#^3bf4c7|universal enveloping algebra]] of $\sl_{2}(\C)$, and then deform it to get the *quantum universal enveloping algebra* $U_{q}(\sl_{2}(\C))$.
Recall from [[General and Special Linear Groups#^4c1fe6|the example]] that $\sl_{2}(\C)$ is the [[Lie Algebra#^5007ba|Lie algebra]] of $2\times 2$ complex traceless matrices, and it has a standard basis $\{e,f,h\}$ with Lie brackets $[H,E]=2E$, $[H,F]=-2F$ and $[E,F]=H$. Its universal enveloping algebra $U(\sl_{2}(\C))$ is a unital associative algebra  generated by $E, F, H$.

> [!definition] $q$-Number
> Suppose $\newcommand{\F}{\mathbb{F}}\F$ is a [[Ring, Field and Integral Domain#^575174|field]], and $q\in\F$ is a nonzero element that is not either $1$ or $-1$. Then the *$q$-number* of an integer $n\in\Z$ is defined as
> $$ [n]_{q}:= \frac{q^{n}-q^{-n}}{q-q^{-1}} = q^{n-1} + q^{n-3} + \cdots+ q^{-n+1}. $$
> 

> [!definition] $\newcommand{\C}{\mathbb{C}}\newcommand{\sl}{\mathfrak{sl}}U_{q}(\sl_{2}(\C))$
> Consider $q\in\C^{\times}$. The *quantum universal enveloping algebra* $U_{q}(\sl_{2}(\C))$ is the unital associative algebra over $\C$ generated by $E, F, K, K^{-1}, L$ subject to the relations:
> $$\begin{gather} KK^{-1}=1,\qquad KEK^{-1}=q^{2}E,\qquad KFK^{-1}=q^{-2}F,\\  [E,F]=L,\qquad (q-q^{-1})L=K-K^{-1}, \\ \qquad [L,E]=q(EK+K^{-1}E), \qquad [L,F]=-q^{-1}(FK+K^{-1}F) . \end{gather}$$

> [!remark]+ $U_{q}(\sl_{2}(\C))$ is deformed from $U(\sl_{2}(\C))$
> When $q=1$, this definition reduces to the usual universal enveloping algebra $U(\sl_{2}(\C))$ with $K$ and $L$ identified with $1$ and $H$ (up to a sign), respectively.
> We don't actually need the generator $L$ if $q\neq 1, -1$. In fact, if $q\neq 1, -1$, then $L=\frac{K-K^{-1}}{q-q^{-1}}$. But we keep $L$ in the definition to easily compare with the classical case $q=1$.

> [!proposition] 
> $U_{q}:=U_{q}(\sl_{2}(\C))$ has a [[Coalgebra and Hopf Algebra#^ad39af|Hopf algebra]] structure with comultiplication $\Delta$, counit $\varepsilon$ and antipode $s$ defined by
> $$\begin{aligned}\Delta\colon U_{q} \to U_{q} \otimes U_{q}, \qquad &E\mapsto 1\otimes E+E\otimes K, \qquad  F\mapsto K^{-1}\otimes F + F\otimes 1, \\ & K\mapsto K\otimes K,\qquad  K^{-1}\mapsto K^{-1}\otimes K^{-1}, \\ & L\mapsto K^{-1}\otimes L + L\otimes K,\\ \varepsilon\colon U_{q}\to \C , \qquad & E,F,L\mapsto 0, \qquad K,K^{-1}\mapsto 1,\\ s\colon U_{q}\to U_{q}, \qquad &E\mapsto -EK^{-1}, \quad F\mapsto -KF,\quad  K\mapsto K^{-1}, \quad K^{-1}\mapsto K, \quad L\mapsto-L. \end{aligned}$$
