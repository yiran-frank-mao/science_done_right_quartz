## Structure of Semisimple Lie Algebras

> [!theorem] Structure Theorem for Semisimple Lie Algebras
> Let $\newcommand{\g}{\mathfrak{g}}\g$ be a Lie algebra over a field $F$ of characteristic $0$. Then, $\g$ is semisimple if and only if there exists unique $m$ and $\mathfrak{a}_{1}, \ldots, \mathfrak{a}_{m} \triangleleft \mathfrak{g}$ simple ideals such that $$\g=\bigoplus_{i=1}^{m} \mathfrak{a}_{i}.$$ ^2a28d6

*Proof*  We prove the theorem by induction on $\operatorname{dim} \mathfrak{g}$, where the base case $\operatorname{dim} \mathfrak{g}=1$ is clear. Suppose the statement is true for all semisimple Lie algebras $\mathfrak{h}$ with $\operatorname{dim} \mathfrak{h}<\operatorname{dim} \mathfrak{g}$. If $\mathfrak{g}$ is simple then it is its own decomposition and we are done. So suppose otherwise and let $(0) \neq \mathfrak{b} \triangleleft \mathfrak{g}$ be a proper ideal. Then $\mathfrak{b}^{\perp}$ is also an ideal, where $$\mathfrak{b}^{\perp}=\left\{x \in \mathfrak{g}: \kappa_{\mathfrak{g}}(x, y)=0, \forall y \in \mathfrak{b}\right\}.$$To show this note that for $x \in \mathfrak{b}^{\perp}$ and $y \in \mathfrak{g}$ we have$$\kappa_{\mathfrak{g}}([x, y], z)=\kappa_{\mathfrak{g}}(x,[y, z])=0, \quad \forall z \in \mathfrak{b}$$since $[y, z] \in \mathfrak{b}$. So, $\left[\mathfrak{b}^{\perp}, \mathfrak{g}\right] \subset \mathfrak{b}^{\perp}$ as required. By Theorem 9.4 we know that $\kappa_{\mathfrak{g}}$ is non-degenerate, so $\operatorname{dim} \mathfrak{b}+\operatorname{dim} \mathfrak{b}^{\perp}=\operatorname{dim} \mathfrak{g}$. This implies $$\mathfrak{g}=\mathfrak{b}\oplus\mathfrak{b}^{\perp}$$We now show that $\mathfrak{b} \cap \mathfrak{b}^{\perp}=(0)$, which will imply that $\mathfrak{g}=\mathfrak{b} \oplus \mathfrak{b}^{\perp}$. Let $\mathfrak{a}=\mathfrak{b} \cap \mathfrak{b}^{\perp}$, then$$\mathfrak{b}, \mathfrak{b}^{\perp} \supset \mathfrak{a} \supset\left[\mathfrak{b}, \mathfrak{b}^{\perp}\right] \supset[\mathfrak{a}, \mathfrak{a}]$$Hence$$\kappa_{\mathfrak{g}}(\mathfrak{a},[\mathfrak{a}, \mathfrak{a}]) \subset \kappa_{\mathfrak{g}}\left(\mathfrak{b}, \mathfrak{b}^{\perp}\right)=0 \Longrightarrow \kappa_{\mathfrak{g}}(\mathfrak{a},[\mathfrak{a}, \mathfrak{a}])=0.$$So $\mathfrak{a}$ is solvable by [[Killing Forms and Cartan's Criteria#^125ec3|Cartan's criterion]], however $\mathfrak{g}$ is semisimple, so $\mathfrak{a}=(0)$ as desired. Thus $\mathfrak{g}=\mathfrak{b} \oplus \mathfrak{b}^{\perp}$, and from the proof we also obtain that $\left[\mathfrak{b}, \mathfrak{b}^{\perp}\right]=0$. We now show that$$\mathfrak{a} \triangleleft \mathfrak{b} \Longrightarrow \mathfrak{a} \triangleleft \mathfrak{g}$$We know that$$\begin{aligned}{[\mathfrak{g}, \mathfrak{a}] } & =\left[\mathfrak{b} \oplus\mathfrak{b}^{\perp}, \mathfrak{a}\right] \\& =[\mathfrak{b}, \mathfrak{a}]+\left[\mathfrak{b}^{\perp}, \mathfrak{a}\right]\end{aligned}$$However since $\left[\mathfrak{b}, \mathfrak{b}^{\perp}\right]=0$ we obtain $\left[\mathfrak{b}^{\perp}, \mathfrak{a}\right] \subset\left[\mathfrak{b}^{\perp}, \mathfrak{b}\right]=0$. Thus $[\mathfrak{g}, \mathfrak{a}]=[\mathfrak{b}, \mathfrak{a}] \subset \mathfrak{a}$ as required. The same argument shows that this is also true for $\mathfrak{b}^{\perp}$. Lastly we show that $\mathfrak{b}$ is semisimple, observe that$$\kappa_{\mathfrak{b}}=\left.\kappa_{\mathfrak{g}}\right|_{\mathfrak{b} \times \mathfrak{b}}$$Then note that there exists $0 \neq x \in \mathfrak{b}$ such that $x \perp \mathfrak{b}$ if and only if $x \perp \mathfrak{b} \oplus \mathfrak{b}^{\perp}=\mathfrak{g}$, so by the non-degeneracy of $\kappa_{\mathfrak{g}}$ no such $x$ exists. So, $\operatorname{Rad}\left(\kappa_{\mathfrak{b}}\right)=(0)$, meaning $\mathfrak{b}$ is semisimple, while a similar argument shows that $\mathfrak{b}^{\perp}$ is also semisimple.
By our induction hypothesis we know since $\mathfrak{b}, \mathfrak{b}^{\perp}$ are semisimple that there exist simple ideals $\mathfrak{b}_{i} \triangleleft \mathfrak{b}$ and $\mathfrak{c}_{i} \triangleleft \mathfrak{b}^{\perp}$ such that $$\begin{aligned}& \mathfrak{b}=\mathfrak{b}_{1} \oplus \cdots \oplus \mathfrak{b}_{k} \\& \mathfrak{b}^{\perp}=\mathfrak{c}_{1} \oplus \cdots \oplus \mathfrak{c}_{m}\end{aligned}$$Further we know that each $\mathfrak{b}_{i}, \mathfrak{c}_{i} \triangleleft \mathfrak{g}$, so we conclude$$\mathfrak{g}=\mathfrak{b} \oplus \mathfrak{b}^{\perp}=\mathfrak{b}_{1} \oplus \cdots \oplus \mathfrak{b}_{k} \oplus \mathfrak{c}_{1} \oplus \cdots \oplus \mathfrak{c}_{m}.$$So $\mathfrak{g}$ is the direct sum of simple ideals as required. This shows that such a decomposition exists.
To show that our decomposition is unique, we consider an arbitrary decomposition (and our existence proof above gives us one such decomposition): $\mathfrak{g}=\bigoplus \mathfrak{a}_{i}$, a direct sum of simple ideals. We will show that if an ideal $\mathfrak{a}$ has $(0) \neq \mathfrak{a} \triangleleft g$, then $\mathfrak{a}=\mathfrak{a}_{i}$ for some $i$. Note that this is sufficient because we can then compare two decompositions and show that they are equivalent, as each component simple ideal is equal to one component simple ideal in the other decomposition.
By the Jacobi identity, $[\mathfrak{a}, \mathfrak{g}] \triangleleft \mathfrak{a}$. Note that this is equivalent to saying that $[[\mathfrak{a}, \mathfrak{g}], \mathfrak{a}] \subset[\mathfrak{a}, \mathfrak{g}]$ and $[[a, \mathfrak{g}], \mathfrak{a}]=-[[\mathfrak{g}, \mathfrak{a}], \mathfrak{a}]-[[\mathfrak{a}, \mathfrak{a}], \mathfrak{g}] \subset[\mathfrak{a}, \mathfrak{g}]$ since $\mathfrak{a}$ ideal and $\mathfrak{a}$ closed under Lie bracket.
Now we have two cases, since $\mathfrak{a}$ is a simple ideal, and $[\mathfrak{a}, \mathfrak{g}] \triangleleft \mathfrak{a},[\mathfrak{a}, \mathfrak{g}]=(0)$ or $[\mathfrak{a}, \mathfrak{g}]=\mathfrak{a}$. The first implies that $\mathfrak{a}$ commutes with $\mathfrak{g}$ and so $\mathfrak{a} \subset Z(\mathfrak{g})$, but $Z(\mathfrak{g})$ is a solvable ideal as it is abelian, and solvable ideals are ( 0 ) in semisimple g . Hence $[\mathfrak{a}, \mathfrak{g}]=\mathfrak{a}$.
Thence, $[\mathfrak{a}, \mathfrak{g}]=\mathfrak{a}=\left[\mathfrak{a}, \bigoplus a_{i}\right]=\bigoplus\left[\mathfrak{a}, \mathfrak{a}_{i}\right]$. By our argument earlier, $\left[\mathfrak{a}, \mathfrak{a}_{i}\right] \triangleleft \mathfrak{a}_{i}$ and $\left[\mathfrak{a}, \mathfrak{a}_{i}\right] \triangleleft \mathfrak{a}$ so by $\mathfrak{a}, \mathfrak{a}_{i}$ simple, $\left[\mathfrak{a}, \mathfrak{a}_{i}\right]=(0)$ or $\mathfrak{a}_{i}$, and if it is the latter, then it is also precisely $\mathfrak{a}$.
Hence there exists unique $i$ such that $\mathfrak{a}=\left[\mathfrak{a}, \mathfrak{a}_{i}\right]=\mathfrak{a}_{i}$ and our uniqueness proof is done. $\square$

> [!corollary] 
> If $\g=\bigoplus_{i=1}^{m} \mathfrak{a}_{i}$ over a field $F$ of [[Fields and Field Extensions#^2bfbd8|characteristic]] $0$ is semisimple, then 
> 1. $[\g,\g]=\g$
> 2. All ideals and homomorphic images of $\g$ are semisimple.
> 3. If $\mathfrak{b}\triangleleft \g$ then $\mathfrak{b}=\bigoplus_{k}\mathfrak{a}_{i_{k}}$.
>$\quad$
> 

## A Summary of Characterizations of Semisimple Lie Algebras

By the structure theorem, we can deduce the following equivalent characterizations of semisimple Lie algebras by induction:

> [!proposition]
> Suppose $\g$ is a finite dimensional Lie algebra over a field of [[Fields and Field Extensions#^2bfbd8|characteristic]] $0$. The following are equivalent:
> 1. $\g$ is semisimple.
> 2. the Killing form is non-degenerate.
> 3. $\g$ has no non-zero abelian ideals.
> 4. $\g$ has no non-zero solvable ideals.
> 5. the radical of $\g$ is zero.
> $\quad$
