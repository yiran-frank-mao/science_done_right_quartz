> [!definition] Symplectic Group
> If $V=F^{2n}$ for $F\in\{\R,\C\}$, we can realize a [[Symplectic Structures#^b558c2|symplectic form]] by defining $\omega(u,v):=u^{\top}Jv$ for $J=\begin{bmatrix}0 & I_{n}\\-I_{n}&0 \end{bmatrix}\in M_{2n}(F)$ being the [[Linear Symplectic Geometry#^8aa313|almost complex structure]]. The *symplectic group* associated to this form is then defined as $$\newcommand{\SP}{\mathrm{Sp}}\SP_{2n}(F):=\{A\in \GL_{2n}(F)\mid A^{\top} J A = J \}.$$ 
> More generally, we write $\SP(V)$ for all symplectic operators $V\to V$. ^fae051

In fact, linear symplectic spaces of the same dimension are unique up to [[Symplectic Structures#^2dbbf4|symplectomorphism]], that is, an isomorphism $\varphi$ that preserves the symplectic form. For details on why this is the case, see [[Linear Symplectic Geometry#^b9c753|proposition]].
