> [!definition] Subgroup Generated by Subsets
> Let $G$ be a group and $X \subset G$ a non-empty subset. *The subgroup generated by $X$* is
> $$ \langle X\rangle:=\{g\in G\mid\exists n\in\mathbb{N}_{0},x_{i}\in X,e_{i}=\pm1,{\mathrm{~such~that~}}g=x_{1}^{e_{1}}\cdots x_{n}^{e_{n}}\} $$
> This is indeed a subgroup: it contains $1 = x^{−1}x$ (alternatively we could include the empty set in the definition and define the empty product to be 1), it is clearly closed under multiplication and inverses.

<u><b>e.g.</b></u>  The [[Permutations and Symmetric Groups#^5b562f|symmetric group]] is generated by adjacent transpositions.

> [!proposition]
> $\langle X\rangle$ is the smallest subgroup containing $X$ with respect to inclusion.


> [!definition] Commutator and Commutator Subgroup
> The *commutator of two elements $a$ and $b$* in a group G is the element $[a,b] = aba^{-1} b^{-1}$. *The commutator subgroup of $G$* is the group generated by all commutators: $$ [G,G] := \langle [a,b]:a,b \in G\rangle $$

> [!proposition]
> Two elements commute if and only if their commutator is the identity.

*Proof*  This is straightforward from the definition.  $\square$

> [!proposition]
> $[G,G]$ is a [[Homomorphisms, Normal Subgroup & Conjugation#^normal|normal subgroup]] of $G$. Moreover, $\operatorname{Ab}(G):=G/[G,G]$ is [[Groups, Order and Subgroups#^6d511a|abelian]].
> 

*Proof*  For any $g, a,b\in G$, we have $g[a,b]g^{-1}=g aba^{-1}b^{-1}g^{-1}=[gag^{-1}, gbg^{-1}]$, so $[G,G]$ is normal. Moreover, for any $x,y\in G$, we have $$ xy[G,G]=xy (y^{-1}x^{-1}yx)[G,G]=yx[G,G],$$so $G/[G,G]$ is abelian. $\square$

## Centre

> [!definition] Centre
> The *center of a group $G$* is the set of elements that commute with every element of $G$. That is
> $$ Z(G):=\{ z\in G \mid \forall g \in G,zg=gz \} $$

> [!proposition]
> The centre $Z(G)$ is a [[Homomorphisms, Normal Subgroup & Conjugation#^normal|normal subgroup]] of $G$.
