> [!definition] CGC Foliation
> Let $M$ be a $2$-dimensional Riemannian manifold. Then the CGC foliation of $M$ is a [[Distributions and Foliations#^4c94fb|foliation]] such that each leaf is a ($1$-dimensional) curve with constant geodesic curvature.

> [!definition] CGC $\O_2$ Action
> The CGC $\O_2$ action is a smooth group action on a smooth Riemannian $2$-manifold $(M,g)$, say $\phi\colon \O_n \times M \to M, (k,p)\mapsto \phi_k(p)$, such that $$ l(\phi_k(p)) = \frac{\alpha_k}{2\pi} \cdot l(p) $$ where $l(p)$ denotes the arc length of $p$ in the CGC leaf, and $\alpha\colon \O_2 \to S^1 \times \{1,-1\}$ is the canonical chart for the Lie group $\O_2$.

> [!remark]
> Geometrically, it means that we find the CGC leaf containing $p\in M$, then if $\newcommand{\SO}{\mathrm{SO}}k\in \SO_2$, then we move $p$ a distance of $\frac{\alpha_k}{2\pi} \cdot l(p)$ along the leaf; otherwise, $k$ is in $\O_2$ but not in $\SO_2$, then go in the opposite direction.

> [!theorem]
> Suppose $(M,g)$ is a Riemannian $2$-manifold. Then $g$ is rotationally-invariant with respect to some $\renewcommand{\O}{\mathrm{O}}\O_2$ group action if and only if it is rotationally-invariant with respect to the CGC $\O_2$ action.
