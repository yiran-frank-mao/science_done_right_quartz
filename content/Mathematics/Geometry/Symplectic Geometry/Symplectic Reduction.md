## Hamiltonian Actions

> [!definition] Symplectic Group Action
> Suppose $(M,\omega)$ is a [[Symplectic Structures#^b558c2|symplectic manifold]]. A [[Lie Groups#^0e5dd8|Lie group]] action on on $M$ is symplectic if it acts on $M$ via [[Symplectic Structures#^2dbbf4|symplectomorphisms]].

> [!definition] Hamiltonian Action
> Suppose $G$ is a Lie group with [[The Exponential Map#^7b21eb|Lie algebra]] $\newcommand{\g}{\mathfrak{g}}\g$. A symplectic $G$-action $\psi$ on $(M,\omega)$ is Hamiltonian if there exists a map $\mu\colon M\to \g^{*}$ such that
> 1. For all $\xi\in\g$, the [[The Exponential Map#^5f72f5|fundamental vector field]] $X^{\xi}$ is the Hamiltonian vector field generated by $\mu^{\xi}$, where $\mu^{\xi}\colon M\to \R$ is given by $$\mu^{\xi}(p)=\langle \mu(p),\xi\rangle.$$
> 2. $\mu$ is $G$-equivariant with respect to the given action of $G$ on $M$ and the coadjoint action $\newcommand{\Ad}{\operatorname{Ad}}\Ad^{*}$ of $G$ on $\g^{*}$: $$\mu\circ \psi_{g}=\Ad^{*}_{g}\circ \mu,\quad \text{for all }g\in G.$$ 
> 
> $(M,\omega,G,\mu)$ is then called a hamiltonian $G$-space and $\mu$ is a moment map. ^d935f6

<u><b>e.g.</b></u>  
- The moment map of the Hamiltonian action of $S^{1}$ or $\R$ on $(M,\omega)$, is a function $\mu\colon M\to \R$, which plays the role of a Hamiltonian function.
- For the case where $G=T^{n}=(S^{1})^{n}$ is an $n$-torus, the coadjoint action is trivial because $\g^{*}\cong \R^{n}$. So the action $\psi$ is Hamiltonian when each restriction $\psi\bigg|_{S^{1}}$ is Hamiltonian in the previous sense. That is, For each basis vector $\partial_{i}$ of $\R^{n}$, the $i$th component $\mu_{i}$ of $\mu$ is a hamiltonian function that is invariant under the action of the torus. Moreover, if $\mu \colon M\to \R^{n}$ is a moment map for a torus action, then clearly any of its translations $\mu + c$ $(c \in \R^{n})$ is also a moment map for that action. Reciprocally, any two moment maps for a given hamiltonian torus action differ by a constant. We will investigate the torus action further in the context of [[Toric Symplectic Manifold#^4025d9|toric symplectic manifolds]].

## Symplectic Reduction

Classical physicists realized that, whenever there is a symmetry group of dimension $k$ acting on a mechanical system, then the number of degrees of freedom for the position and momenta of the particles may be reduced by $2k$. Symplectic reduction formulates this feature mathematically.

> [!theorem] Marsden-Weinstein-Meyer Theorem
> Let $(M,\omega,G,\mu)$ be a Hamiltonian $G$-space for a [[Compactness of Topological Space#^da2511|compact]] Lie group $G$. Let $i\colon \mu^{-1}(0)\hookrightarrow M$ be the inclusion map. Assume that $G$ acts freely on $\mu^{-1}(0)$. Then
> 1. the orbit space $M_{\mathrm{red}} = \mu^{-1}(0)/G$ is a manifold,
> 2. $\pi\colon \mu^{-1}(0)\to M_{\mathrm{red}}$ is a principal $G$-bundle, and
> 3. there is a symplectic form $\omega_{\mathrm{red}}$ on $M_{\mathrm{red}}$ satisfying $i^{*}\omega = \pi^{*}\omega_{\mathrm{red}}$.
> 
> The pair $(M_{\mathrm{red}},\omega_{\mathrm{red}})$ is called the symplectic reduction of $(M,\omega)$ with respect to $G$ and $\mu$.
