## Topological Braids

> [!definition] Braid
> Fix a collection of $n$ points $z_{1},\dots,z_{n}$ in the unit disk$D$, a braid on $n$ strands is a smooth embedding $\beta\colon I_{1} \sqcup I_{2} \sqcup \dots \sqcup I_{n}\to D$ such that $\beta_{i}(0)=z_{i}$ and $\beta_{i}(1)=z_{s(i)}$ for some permutation $s\colon \{ 1,2,\dots,n \} \to \{ 1,2,\dots,n \}$. 
> ![braids_def.png|150](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/yiran-frank-mao/image_repo/master/Obsidian/braids_def.png) ^311e98

> [!definition] Braid Equivalence
> Two braids are equivalent if they are ambient isotopic in $I\times D^{2}$.

## Braid Group

> [!proposition] The Braid Group
> Set of all braids $B_{n}$ up to equivalence forms a group under the operation of stacking braids.

> [!theorem] Artin's Theorem: The Braid Relation
> The braid group $B_{n}$ is generated by the set of $n$-braids $\sigma_{1},\sigma_{2},\dots,\sigma_{n-1}$ with the following relations: $$B_{n}\cong \left\langle \sigma_{1},\sigma_{2},\dots,\sigma_{n-1} \mid  \sigma_{i}\sigma_{j}=\sigma_{j}\sigma_{i} \text{ for all } |i-j|\geq 2, \sigma_{i}\sigma_{i+1}\sigma_{i}=\sigma_{i+1}\sigma_{i}\sigma_{i+1} \right\rangle$$

> [!proposition]
> $B_{2}\cong \Z$.

*Proof*  The braid group on $2$ strands is generated by $\sigma_{1}$ without any relation. Thus $B_{2}\cong \Z$.

## The Word Problem

> [!definition] The Word Problem
> The word problem for a group presented by a system of generators and relations, in particular for the braid group, is to find an algorithm that determines, for any pair of words in the generators of the group, whether or not they present the same element (i.e., whether or not one can be taken to the other by applying the group's relations and the trivial relations). The word problem (which of course is always solvable for commutative groups) is solvable for the braid group $B_{n}$ for any $n$.

> [!definition] Fully Reduced Word 
> A word $w$ is said to be completely reduced if, for any integer $i$, any occurrence of the letter $x_{i}$ is separated from any occurrence of the letter $x_{i}^{-1}$ by at least one occurrence of a letter $x_{j}^{\pm 1}$ with $j<i$.

<u><b>e.g.</b></u>  $b_{2}b_{1}b_{2}^{-1}$ is fully reduced, $b_{2}b_{3}b_{2}^{-1}$ is not.

> [!proposition]
> Any element $\beta\in B_{n}$ can be represented by a fully reduced word of generators.

*Proof*  Suppose $\Sigma=\{\sigma_{1},\sigma_{2},\dots,\sigma_{n-1}\}$ generates $B_{n}$. And $w\in W(\overline{\Sigma})$ represents some $\beta\in B_{n}$. If $w$ is not reduced, then it is of the form $w=w_{0} \sigma_{i}^{e}w_{1}\sigma_{i}^{-e} w_{2}$ with $e=\pm 1$, $w_{0}$ is completely reduced, and $w_{1}$ contains only $\sigma_{j}$ with $j>i$. 

> [!algorithm] The Dehornoy Algorithm
> Given two words of the generators of $B_{n}$, apply elementary reductions to both words until they are completely reduced. Then compare the two completely reduced words to see if they are equal.
