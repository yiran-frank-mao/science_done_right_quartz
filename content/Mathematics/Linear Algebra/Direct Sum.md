**Def**  <i><u>Sum of Subsets</u></i>
Let $U_{1},U_{2},\dots,U_{k}$ be subsets of $V$, the sum of $U_{1},U_{2}\dots,U_{k}$ is the set of all sums of the elements in $U_{1},U_{2}\dots,U_{k}$. That is $U_{1}+U_{2}+\dots U_{k}=\{u_{1}+u_{2}+\dots+u_{k}\mid u_{i}\in U_{i}\}$.

**Prop**  If the subsets are subspaces, then the sum of these subsets is a subspace.

**Def**  <i><u>Direct Sum</u></i>
Given a collection of subspaces $U_{1},U_{2}\dots,U_{k}$, $U_{1}+U_{2}+\dots U_{k}$ is a direct sum if each element can be expressed as a sum $u_{1}+u_{2}+\dots u_{k}$ in a unique way. And if so, we write $U_{1}\oplus\ U_{2}\oplus\dots \oplus U_{k}$. ^2700ee

<u><b>e.g.</b></u>  $\R^3$ is the direct sum of the $x$-$y$ plane and $z$-axis.
