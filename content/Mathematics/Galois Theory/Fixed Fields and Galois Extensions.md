## Field Extension Automorphisms

> [!definition] Galois Group
> Suppose $K\supset F$ is a finite field extension. Then the Galois group $\Gal(K/F)$ is the group of all $F$-automorphisms of $K$, where an $F$-automorphism is a [[Fields and Field Extensions#^72b6c5|field extension isomorphism]] $K\to K$.

> [!definition] Galois Extension
> A finite extension $K\supset F$ is a Galois extension if the order of its Galois group $\Gal(K/F)$ is equal to the degree of the extension $[K:F]$.

<u><b>e.g.</b></u>  The field of complex numbers $\C$ is a Galois extension of the field of real numbers $\R$, with Galois group $\Gal(\C/\R)$ being a cyclic group of order two, generated by the complex conjugation.

> [!lemma]
> Let $K\supset F$ be a finite field extension. If a polynomial $f\in F[x]$ is irreducible, then $\Gal(K/F)$ acts [[Group Actions#^065d30|transitively]] on its roots.

## Fixed Fields

> [!theorem] Fixed Field Theorem
> Let $H$ be a finite group of automorphisms of a field $\newcommand{\Gal}{\operatorname{Gal}}K$, and let $F=K^{H}$ be its fixed field. Then $K$ is a finite extension of $F$, and its degree $[K:F]=|H|$, which is the [[Groups, Order and Subgroups#^3bce31|order]] of $H$.
> 

<u><b>e.g.</b></u>

> [!theorem] Lüroth's theorem
> Let $F$ be a subfield of the field $\C(t)$ of rational functions that contains $\C$ and is not $\C$ itself. Then $F$ is isomorphic to a field $\C(u)$ of rational functions.

## Galois Extensions

> [!definition] Intermediate Field
> If $K$ is a field extension of $F$, an intermediate field $L$ is a field such that $F\subset L\subset K$. An intermediate field is proper if $L\neq F$ and $L\neq K$.

> [!theorem] Characterization of Galois Extensions
> Let $K\supset F$ be a finite extension and let $G$ be its Galois group. The following are equivalent:
> - $K\supset F$ is a Galois extension;
> - The fixed field $K^{G}$ is equal to $F$;
> - $K$ is the splitting field over $F$.
>$\quad$

> [!corollary]
> One can deduce the following properties from the above theorem:
> - Every finite extension $K\supset F$ is contained in a Galois extension.
> - If $K\supset F$ is a Galois extension with Galois group $G$, and if $L$ is an intermediate field, then $K$ is also a Galois extension of $L$, and the Galois group $H$ of $K$ over $L$ is a [[Groups, Order and Subgroups#^1ccb07|subgroup]] of $G$.
>$\quad$

*Proof*  The first statement holds because every finite field extension is contained in a splitting field, which is a Galois extension. For the second statement, if $K\supset F$ is a Galois extension with Galois group $G$, then $K$ is a splitting field over $F$ and $F=K^{G}$. Suppose $L$ is an intermediate field, then $K$ must also be a splitting field over $L$, thus $K\supset L$ is also Galois. Let its Galois group be $H$, then $L=K^{H}$. So $K^{G}\subset K^{H}$, which means every automorphism in $H$ must fix every element in $K^{G}$, thus $H\subset G$, and furthermore, $H\leq G$. $\square$

## The Main Theorem

> [!theorem] Main Theorem of Galois Theory
> Let $K\supset F$ be a Galois extension. Let $G$ be its Galois group. There is a one-to-one correspondence between the subgroups of $G$ and the intermediate fields $L$ such that $F\subset L\subset K$. The correspondence is given by $$H\mapsto K^{H},$$where $K^{H}$ is the fixed field of $H$, or conversely $L\mapsto \Gal(K/L)$, sending an intermediate field $L$ to its Galois group.

> [!theorem] 
> Let $K\supset F$ be a Galois extension with Galois group $G$, and let $L$ be the fixed field of a subgroup $H\leq G$. The extension $L\supset F$ is a Galois extension if and only if $H$ is a [[Homomorphisms, Normal Subgroup & Conjugation#^normal|normal subgroup]] of $G$. In this case, the Galois group $\Gal(L/F)$ is isomorphic to the quotient group $G/H$.
