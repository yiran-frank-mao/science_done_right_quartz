


## Mean Value Theorems

> [!theorem] Lagrange Mean Value Theorem
> If $f\colon [a,b]\to \R$ is continuous on $[a,b]$ and differentiable on $(a,b)$, then there exists $c\in (a,b)$ such that $$f'(c)=\frac{f(b)-f(a)}{b-a}.$$ ^1a7318


> [!theorem] Cauchy Mean Value Theorem
> If $f,g\colon [a,b]\to \R$ are continuous on $[a,b]$ and differentiable on $(a,b)$, then there exists $c\in (a,b)$ such that $$(f(b)-f(a))g'(c)=(g(b)-g(a))f'(c).$$
