## Introduction

Geometric measure theory is a mathematical discipline that combines techniques from geometry, measure theory, and analysis to study geometric properties of sets and measures in Euclidean space and beyond. Central to the theory are concepts such as Hausdorff measures, rectifiable sets, and currents, which provide rigorous frameworks for analyzing the size, shape, and regularity of intricate geometric structures. Developed initially to tackle challenging problems in calculus of variations, minimal surfaces, and geometric analysis, geometric measure theory has since evolved into a fundamental tool across various branches of mathematics, including partial differential equations, optimal transport, and geometric topology.
