## Introduction
Classical statistics is a branch of mathematics that deals with the analysis of data and the inference of patterns, relationships, and effects from data. Classical statistics uses techniques such as hypothesis testing, estimation, confidence intervals, and regression to draw conclusions from data based on assumptions and models. Classical statistics is also known as frequentist statistics, because it relies on the concept of frequency or probability of events occurring in the long run.
