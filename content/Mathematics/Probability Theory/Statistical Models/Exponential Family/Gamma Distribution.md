**Def**  <i><u>Gamma Distribution</u></i>
Given parameters shape $\alpha>0$ and rate $\beta>0$, the distribution density function for gamma distribution is $$f(x\mid \alpha,\beta)=\frac{x^{\alpha-1}e^{-\beta x}\beta^\alpha}{\Gamma(\alpha)}\quad\text{for }x>0$$where $\Gamma$ is the gamma function, written as $X\sim\mathrm{Gamma}(\alpha,\beta)$.

**Prop**  The mean of gamma distribution is given by the division of its shape and rate parameters: $\mu=\alpha/\beta$. The variance is $\sigma^2=\alpha/\beta^2$.
