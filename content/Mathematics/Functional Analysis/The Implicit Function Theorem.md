> [!theorem] Implicit Function Theorem
> Let $X$, $Y$ and $Z$ be [[Banach Spaces#^7196a5|Banach spaces]], $f\colon X\times Y\to Z$ be Fréchet differentiable at $(x_0, y_0)$, and $f(x_0, y_0) = 0$. If $y\mapsto \mathrm{D}f(x_{0},y_{0})(0,y)$ is a Banach space isomorphism from $Y$ to $Z$, then there exist open neighborhoods $U$ of $x_0$ and $V$ of $y_0$ and a unique Fréchet differentiable function $g\colon U\to V$ such that $$f(x,g(x))=0, f(x,y)=0 \iff y=g(x), \text{ for all } (x,y)\in U\times V$$
