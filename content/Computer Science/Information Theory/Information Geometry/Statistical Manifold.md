## Statistical Manifold

>[!definition] Amari-Chentsov Tensor
>Given a pair of [[Conjugate Connections#^98fcf9|conjugate connections]] $\nabla$ and $\tilde{\nabla}$ on $(M,g)$, the Amari-Chentsov tensor is a $(0,3)$-tensor defined by$$C_{ijk}:=\Gamma_{ijk}-\tilde{\Gamma}_{ijk}$$or in coordinate-free form, $$C(X,Y,Z):=\langle\nabla_XY-\tilde{\nabla}_{X}Y,Z\rangle $$

>[!proposition]
>The Amari-Chentsov tensor is totally symmetric if the conjugate connections are torsion-free.

*Proof*  Since both connections are torsion-free, it is easy to show that $C(X,Y,Z)=C(Y,X,Z)$. By conjugation, we have $$X{g}(Y,Z)=g(\nabla_XY,Z)+g(Y,\bar{\nabla}_{X}Z),\text{~for all~}X,Y,Z\in\mathcal{X}(M)$$ Now consider $$ \begin{aligned}
C(X,Z,Y)&=\langle \nabla_{X}Z-\bar{\nabla}_{X}Z, Y\rangle\\
&=\langle \nabla _{X}Z,Y \rangle - \langle\bar{\nabla}_{X}Z,Y\rangle \\
 &= X\langle Y,Z\rangle -\langle Z,\bar{\nabla}_{X}Y\rangle - X\langle Z, Y\rangle+\langle \nabla_{X}Y, Z\rangle\\
&= \langle \nabla_{X} Y - \bar{\nabla}_{X}Y, Z\rangle\\
&= C(X,Y,Z)
\end{aligned}$$Therefore since [[Symmetric Group#^013f0e|any symmetric group is generated by adjacent transpositions]], the Amari-Chentsov tensor is totally symmetric. $\square$

>[!definition] Statistical Manifold
>A statistical manifold $(M, g, \nabla, \tilde{\nabla}, C)$ is a manifold $M$ equipped with a [[Riemannian Metrics#^c3ac13|metric tensor]] $g$, a pair of [[Torsion and Curvature#^bec0c5|torsion-free]] [[Conjugate Connections#^98fcf9|conjugate connections]] $(\nabla, \tilde{\nabla})$ and corresponding totally symmetric Amari-Chentsov $C$. ^f45770

## $\alpha$-Connections

>[!definition] $\alpha$-Connection
>For any statistical manifold $(M,g, ∇, \tilde{\nabla} ,C)$, we can define a family of connections $\{\nabla^{\alpha}\}_{\alpha\in\R}$ called $\alpha$-connections, such that $$g(\nabla_X^\alpha Y,Z)=g(\nabla^{\mathrm{LC}}_XY,Z)+\frac\alpha2C(X,Y,Z)\text{ for all }X,Y,Z\in\mathfrak{X}(M)$$

>[!proposition]
>$\nabla^{\alpha}$ is [[Torsion and Curvature#^bec0c5|torsion free]] for any $\alpha\in\R$.

*Proof*  Choose some coordinate chart, then we can derive the coordinate form: $$\begin{aligned} \Gamma_{ij,k}^{\alpha}=\Gamma_{ij,k}^{\mathrm{LC}}-\frac\alpha2C_{ij,k} \\ \Gamma_{ij,k}^{-\alpha} = \Gamma_{ij,k}^{\mathrm{LC}}+\frac\alpha2C_{ij,k}\end{aligned}$$where $\Gamma_{ki,j}=\sum_{l}\Gamma_{ij}^lg_{lk}$. Observe that they don't change if swap indices $i$ and $j$, thus they are torsion free by [[Torsion and Curvature#^50eeae|theorem]]. $\square$

>[!proposition]
>For any fixed $\alpha\in \R$, $(\nabla^{\alpha}, \nabla^{-\alpha})$ forms a pair of conjugate connection.

*Proof* $$\begin{aligned}&\langle\nabla^{\alpha}_{X}Y, Z\rangle + \langle y, \nabla^{-\alpha}_{X} Z\rangle\\=& \langle\nabla^{\mathrm{LC}}_{X}Y, Z\rangle +\frac{\alpha}{2} C(X,Y,Z) +\langle \nabla^{\mathrm{LC}}_{X}Z, Y\rangle-\frac{\alpha}{2}C(Y,X,Z)\\=& \langle\nabla^{\mathrm{LC}}_{X}Y, Z\rangle + \langle Y, \nabla^{\mathrm{LC}}_{X}Z\rangle\\=& X\langle Y,Z\rangle \end{aligned}$$The last equation holds since the Levi-Civita connection is self-conjugate. $\square$

Therefore for any $\alpha\in\R$, $(M,g,\nabla^{\alpha}, \nabla^{-\alpha})$ forms a statistical manifold with totally symmetric cubic tensor $C$ being defined correspondingly.

> [!proposition]
> The $α$-connections $∇^{\alpha}$ can also be constructed directly from a pair $(∇, \tilde{\nabla})$ of conjugate connections by taking the following weighted combination: $$\nabla^{\alpha} = \frac{1+\alpha}{2} \nabla + \frac{1-\alpha}{2} \tilde{\nabla}$$
